# Part 1 — The Smallest Viable Audience

> Seth's rule: pick the smallest group you can win, then dominate it. "Ambitious
> businesses" is everyone, which means it's no one. Here is the narrowest real
> market BAZ can own.

## Audience (one sentence)
**AI-native and software founders doing $20k–$250k/mo who build the product
themselves and refuse to babysit a junior-staffed agency.**

## Why this group
- They are technical (matches the founder's actual edge: AI dev, Linux/security, CRM).
- They already distrust generalist agencies — we don't have to convince them, only prove it.
- They ship fast and respect speed; our model ("no juniors, no learning curves") is the product.
- They have budget but not time — a flat, done-for-them offer converts.

## What they hate (our enemy in the story)
1. Agencies that send decks instead of results.
2. Generalists who guess at their market.
3. Spreading thin across 5 channels and getting noise.

## What they want
One senior team that builds the whole marketing system and gets out of the way.

## Who is NOT the audience (say no loudly)
- Enterprise procurement with committees and 3-quote processes.
- Pure brand/logo plays with no performance pressure.
- Anyone who needs to be educated on why focus wins.

## The one hero offer (Part 2)
**"Your marketing system, built and running in 45 days."**
Flat $9,500 + 10% of attributed lift for 90 days. One channel. One funnel.
Live in 45 days or you don't pay.

The other 13 services still exist and get used — but they are never the
front door. Breadth is the tell of someone afraid to pick.
