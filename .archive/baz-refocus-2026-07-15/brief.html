The one brief · for the one offer

The creative brief: 45-day marketing system.

One offer, one audience, one channel. Ogilvy's discipline, Godin's scope. If a brief needs a second page of targets, it's broken.

The product

Your marketing system, built and running in 45 days. One senior team, one channel, one funnel.

The prospect

Technical / AI-native founders doing $20k–$250k/mo who build the product and hate junior-staffed agencies.

The promise

Live in 45 days — or you don't pay. $9,500 + 10% of attributed lift, first 90 days.

The enemy

Agencies that send decks instead of results; generalists who guess; spread-thin "be everywhere" marketing.

The single most important thing to say

"We build your entire marketing system and have it live in 45 days — senior-only, or you don't pay."

Support points (in order)

  1. One senior team — no juniors, no learning curves.
  2. One channel mastered before a second is touched.
  3. Built on "Intensity beats extensity" — focus is the strategy.

The tone

Plain, confident, specific, a little contrarian. Talk to a founder like a founder.

The media (one channel, per the playbook)

Founder-led email + LinkedIn (owned + earned). One hero essay per week. Paid only after the channel compounds.

What success looks like

calls_booked_from_list / subscribers moves. Not impressions. Not followers.
