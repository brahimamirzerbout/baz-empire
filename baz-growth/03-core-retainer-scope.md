# 03 — Core retainer scope (productized)

> The productized Core retainer: $6.5–9.5k/mo, 90-day minimum, one channel.
> This is the revenue engine. Everything is scoped, named, and exit-criterized
> — no bespoke quoting, no scope creep, no junior pod.

## Offer at a glance

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Tier** | Core |
| **Price** | $6,500 / $8,000 / $9,500 per month (band by media spend — see grid) |
| **Term** | 90-day minimum, then month-to-month |
| **Scope** | ONE channel (paid media: Google OR Meta OR TikTok) + tracking + weekly demo |
| **Delivery** | Senior partner on the account. No junior pods. |
| **Geography** | MENA / EU / US (calibrated) |
| **Exit criterion** | Day-90 review against the targets below. Renew, expand to Growth, or graduate. |

## Pricing band (by media spend)

| Client monthly media spend | Core retainer | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| $20k–$100k | $6,500/mo | one channel, lean management load |
| $100k–$300k | $8,000/mo | more creative + iteration cycles |
| $300k–$500k | $9,500/mo | full senior-partner load, weekly optimization |

> > $500k/mo spend → graduate to Growth tier ($18–28k/mo, 3–5 partners, full
> > integrated stack). Do not oversell Growth to a Core-fit client.

## What's included (the productized scope)

| # | Deliverable | Frequency | Platform hook |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Server-side tracking setup** — GTM server + GA4 + CAPI for the scoped channel | day 1–14 (once) | `geo.ts` doctrine + integration adapters |
| 2 | **Budget reallocation** — ROAS-weighted reallocator run on client data | weekly | `/api/performance/optimize` |
| 3 | **Weekly demo** — live numbers, not a status report; one decision per week | weekly | client portal (WIP) + KPI dashboard (`05`) |
| 4 | **Creative iteration** — 2–4 ad variants tested per cycle against the reallocated budget | per cycle | `AdMetric` logging |
| 5 | **Monthly revenue report** — LTV/CAC/payback, not impressions | monthly | KPI dashboard (`05`) |
| 6 | **One named senior partner** + Slack/Teams access during business hours | continuous | senior-partner model |
| 7 | **CSV export of all metrics** — client owns their data, always | on demand | hub CSV export (Phase 1) |

## What's NOT included (the fence)

- Additional channels (Google + Meta = Growth tier, not Core).
- SEO / content / influencer / web build (separate project or Growth tier).
- Creative production (photo/video) — referral or project add-on.
- Landing-page builds beyond the tracked conversion path.
- 24/7 response (business-hours SLA; Growth tier gets priority).

> The fence is the offer. Scope creep kills the senior-partner margin and the
> 90-day exit criterion. Refer anything over the fence to the Growth tier or
> a fixed project.

> If tracking shows the channel can't hit the targets → graduate the client

## The pitch in one line (for the proposal)

> "Core is one channel, one senior partner, and a weekly demo where we move
> your budget toward the dollars that actually make money. Ninety days, then
> we decide together whether to keep going."

## Assumptions / flags

- All day-90 targets are **composite** until pilot 1 ships. The real baseline
  gets set in week 2 of the first engagement (after tracking goes live).
- The pricing band assumes the client's spend is verifiable in discovery.
  Do not quote a band before the audit.
- This is a **retainer**, not a SaaS subscription. The guardrail holds.
