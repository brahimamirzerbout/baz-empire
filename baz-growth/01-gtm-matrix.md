## The pilot ICP (one profile)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Business type** | DTC / e-commerce brand OR a scale-up B2B SaaS with paid-media spend |
| **Stage** | Post-product-fit, $50k–$500k/mo media spend, paying for ads but not measuring payback |
| **Pain** | "We spend on Google/Meta/TikTok but can't tell which dollar made money." ROAS is guessed, not measured. |
| **Buyer** | Founder / CMO / Head of Growth — the person who owns the revenue number |
| **Geography** | Tier 1: MENA (Algiers-base advantage, AR/FR). Tier 2: EU (GDPR-ready, EN). Tier 3: US (EN, case-hungry). |
| **Budget** | Can absorb $6.5–9.5k/mo Core retainer + media spend without flinching |
| **Disqualifiers** | Pre-product-fit; < $20k/mo spend (no ROAS signal to optimize); wants SaaS not a partner; "we just need someone to post content" |

## Why this ICP

- They have **media spend** → the performance engine has real input to
  reallocate. No spend = no ROAS = no demo.
- They **can't measure payback** → BAZ's tracking-is-the-moat doctrine is the
  exact gap. The fix is the offer.
- The **buyer owns revenue** → the conversation is about LTV/CAC/payback, not
  impressions. That's BAZ's home court.
- **Post-product-fit** → the money BAZ makes them is real revenue, not vanity.

## Assumptions / flags

- The ICP assumes BAZ sells to **brands**. If BAZ instead sells the hub to
  **agencies**, the ICP becomes "mid-market agency, 5–20 staff, operations
  pain" and the wedge is the hub-as-substrate, not performance services.
  **Confirm the model before building the list.**
- Spend estimates are directional until verified in discovery. Do not quote
  them back to the prospect.
