# 02 — Marketplace Uses Real Named Marketers as "Hireable": Right-of-Publicity + IP + False Advertising

**Severity:** CRITICAL
**Dimension:** IP / Right of publicity / False advertising
**Status:** CONFIRMED (live-site fetch)

## What it is
The live site markets a "Marketplace":
- "Hire Seth, Gary, PBD, Kotler, Ogilvy, Schwartz — plus 6 operators. Everyone eats."
- "12+ Marketers in marketplace: Seth, Gary, PBD, Kotler, Ogilvy, Schwartz…"
- All inside the $99/mo product.

These are real, identifiable people:
- **Living:** Seth Godin, Gary Vaynerchuk, Patrick Bet-David, Philip Kotler.
- **Deceased:** David Ogilvy (d. 2019), Eugene Schwartz (d. 2007) — estates may hold rights/trademarks in their names.

The site invites users to "hire" them for $99/mo. You cannot hire Seth Godin for $99/mo, and the deceased's names/likenesses are used without estate permission.

## How the enemy perceives and exploits it
- **Right of publicity / IP (the one-email kill):** living marketers have right-of-publicity — commercial use of name/likeness without consent is actionable. Deceased: estates (Ogilvy, Schwartz) may hold publicity-adjacent rights and/or trademarks in their names. A competitor sends one email to any of them or their lawyer → cease-and-desist, takedown, possible damages. This is the single cheapest kill shot against BAZ in the entire dossier.
- **False advertising:** "Hire Seth Godin" implies Seth Godin will work for the buyer. He will not. FTC §5 (deceptive acts) + UK ASA / EU consumer-authority equivalents. A competitor files a complaint; regulators can order takedown + fines.
- **Trust / positioning:** any sophisticated buyer sees "hire Seth Godin for $99/mo" and instantly downgrades BAZ to hype-shop — the precise opposite of "company-grade," and a direct violation of BAZ's "never hype/growth-hacking shop" doctrine. It signals unseriousness louder than any claim on the site.

## How we solve it
Stop using real people's names as hireable agents.
- **Option A (recommended):** Rename marketplace agents to the original role-marks the site already uses — "The Strategist," "The Storyteller," "The Copywriter," "The Analyst," "The PR," "The Researcher." Attribute the lineage editorially/educationally ("built on the work of Godin, Hormozi, PBD, Kotler, Schwartz") — ideas/frameworks aren't copyrightable, but never imply you can *hire* the actual person and never imply their endorsement.
- **Option B (impractical):** obtain written permission/licensing from each living person + each deceased's estate. Slow, expensive, unlikely — not the move for a $99/mo product.

## Sequence
1. Founder approves Option A — 48h.
2. Operator removes all real-name "hire" language from the live site: marketplace section, homepage, FAQ, and source (`roi-brand.ts`, agent definitions, lexicon).
3. Replace with role-based agent names + a factual, non-endorsement "built on the lineage" credit.
4. Audit the lexicon/library/case-studies for any other real-name commercial-use or implied-endorsement claims; fix uniformly.

## Exit criterion
Zero surfaces say "hire [real person's name]." Verify: re-fetch the live site + grep source for {Godin, Vaynerchuk, Bet-David, Kotler, Ogilvy, Schwartz} — none appear as hireable. Educational attribution remains, with no commercial-hire or endorsement implication.

## Risk if unfixed
A single C&D or regulator complaint forces takedown + damages + a reputational hit; enterprise buyers dismiss BAZ on sight.
