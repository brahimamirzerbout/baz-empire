# 03 — Ogilvy (the agency)

> Ogilvy & Mather → Ogilvy. The research-and-Big-Idea agency David Ogilvy built. Now part
> of WPP.

## Bio [fact]

- 1948: founded as **Hewitt, Ogilvy, Benson & Mather** (NY), backed by London's Mather &
  Crowther (run by David's brother Francis)
- Renamed **Ogilvy & Mather**; 1965 merged with Mather & Crowther → international; 1966
  IPO (one of the first ad firms to go public)
- Grew to top-12 US agency; clients: Lever Brothers, General Foods, American Express,
  Shell (entire North America account), Sears (first national campaign), Rolls-Royce,
  Dove, IBM, KLM, Pepperidge Farm
- 1989: **WPP** (Martin Sorrell) acquired Ogilvy Group for $864M (hostile); David Ogilvy
  named non-exec chairman
- Now **Ogilvy** (rebranded, WPP); one of the world's largest agency networks
- Sister WPP agencies: GroupM, VML (post-2023 merger of VMLY&R + Wunderman Thompson)

## The agency's signature moves [fact]

- Research-first methodology institutionalized (Ogilvy came from Gallup)
- Long-copy, big-idea brand-building (Hathaway, Schweppes, Rolls-Royce, Dove)
- "We sell or else" — Ogilvy's late-in-life direct-response partisanship
- Training programs ("Magic Lanterns") codified into institutional knowledge
- 2024-25: WPP invested £250M→£300M in **WPP Open**, its AI-powered marketing OS — won
  Amazon + Unilever on the back of it (L.E.K. moat #1 in action)

## Core lessons [fact]

1. **The founder's principles outlive the founder when institutionalized.** Ogilvy's
   research + Big Idea discipline survived the WPP acquisition because it was codified
   into training and process, not just David's head.
2. **The proprietary-tech moat is now the agency-level differentiator.** WPP Open is the
   2025 proof: an agency OS that wins holding-company-scale accounts. PMG's Alli ($6B
   media) is the independent equivalent.
3. **Branding + direct response are not enemies** — Ogilvy built brand *through* direct
   response (ran DR newspaper ads to generate leads early on) and ended life praising DR
   over "creativity-worship." BAZ's "revenue not vanity" is this lineage.
4. **IPO/exit changes the game.** Going public enabled scale but invited the hostile
   takeover. For BAZ: independence > IPO until the moat is institutionalized.

## BAZ application

- **Institutionalize the founder's principles or they die with the founder.** BAZ's
  doctrine (revenue not vanity, tracking is the moat, 90-day plans, performance-first
  web) must be codified into SOPs + the Operator Agent (Hormozi ACQ pattern) so it
  survives Brahim stepping back. This is Ogilvy's training-programs lesson + L.E.K.'s
  institutional-relationships moat.
- **The proprietary-tech moat at boutique scale = BAZ's tracking stack + Operator Agent.**
  WPP Open costs £300M; BAZ's version costs near-zero marginal (built by the AI agent).
  Same moat, different cost basis — the unfair edge.
- **Branding through direct response:** BAZ builds the "operator-who-ships" *brand*
  through *direct-response* content + outbound, not through image ads. Ogilvy's method,
  BAZ's medium.
- **Stay independent until the moat is institutionalized, then the decision is optional.**
  Premature exit kills the culture that built the moat (Droga5-acquisition caution +
  W+K-trust model).
