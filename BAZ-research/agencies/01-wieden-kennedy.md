# 01 — Wieden+Kennedy

> The largest independent advertising agency in the world. Culture + independence as moat.
> Founded Portland, OR, April 1, 1982 — by Dan Wieden & David Kennedy.

## Bio [fact]

- Founded by **Dan Wieden** (1945–2022) & **David Kennedy** (d. 2022) in Portland, OR
- Met 1980 at William Cain agency working on Nike; took Nike as founding client in 1982
- First ads: 3 Nike TV spots during the 1982 NYC Marathon (Nike's first nationwide
  broadcasts)
- Offices: Portland, NYC, London, Amsterdam, Shanghai, Tokyo, Delhi, São Paulo
- **2015:** put all shares into a trust "whose only obligation is to never ever under any
  circumstances sell the agency" — independence permanently secured
- "An ad agency that didn't like advertising"; culture of eccentricity, Founder's Day
  (April 1) company-wide art/camping/films

## Core lessons [fact]

1. **Independence is the moat.** No holdco pressure → long bets, culture retention, work
   that makes a cultural dent. Wieden engineered this permanently via the 2015 trust.
2. **Make advertising safe for entertainment.** Reason-why product-difference marketing
   was dead (commoditization) — W+K made attitude/entertainment the differentiator.
3. **"Just walk in stupid every day."** (Wieden/Kennedy's only dictum worth heeding.)
   Beginner's mind over expertise lock-in.
4. **Culture is a recruiting + retention weapon.** The "ragtag army" + Founder's Day
   produces work that a buttoned-up holdco couldn't.
5. **The tagline as cultural artifact** — "Just Do It" outlived every campaign it capped.
6. **Outsider status as positioning** — Portland, not NYC; rebels, not suits.

## BAZ application

- **Independence = BAZ's structural advantage.** Boutique, senior-partner, no holdco,
  no junior pods → BAZ can take the long bet (build the moat product) that a holdco
  couldn't justify. Protect it; don't take VC that would force a flip.
- **"Just walk in stupid every day"** = BAZ's resistance to expertise lock-in. Every
  engagement starts with the Schwartz awareness diagnosis, not a templated playbook —
  even when the playbook worked last time.
- **Culture as retention weapon:** BAZ's senior-partner model is only durable if the
  culture keeps senior people (3.4yr retention at 8-figure agencies vs 2.1yr). Founder's
  Day equivalent: build the internal rituals that make BAZ the place senior operators
  *stay*.
- **The cultural dent, scaled down:** BAZ won't make a Super Bowl ad, but the
  "operator-who-ships" POV content is W+K's "make a dent" principle at boutique scale —
  work that says something, not work that fills space.
