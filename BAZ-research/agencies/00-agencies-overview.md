## The 5 moats separating top ad agencies from the rest (L.E.K. 2025) [fact]

| Moat | What it is | BAZ alignment |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Proprietary technology & data | Tools competitors can't match; measurable performance gains; scale without proportional headcount | ⚠️ **BAZ's biggest untapped leverage.** Founder is the builder → the moat is buildable at near-zero marginal cost |
| 2. Specialized service expertise | AI-enabled automation, analytics, performance media, retail/CTV, influencer commerce | ✅ BAZ paid + tracking stack = specialized service expertise |
| 3. Vertical & segment expertise | Deep credibility in a vertical or client-size segment (SMB/midmarket) | ⚠️ BAZ sharpening ICP-by-posture (Godin category) rather than vertical |
| 4. Institutional client relationships | Multi-stakeholder, multi-service, survives a contact leaving; ~97% retention; 8-12x EBITDA multiples | ⚠️ BAZ relationships currently founder-dependent → institutionalize |
| 5. M&A + integration capability | Documented integration playbook | Out of scope for boutique BAZ — note only |

**Examples:** WPP Open (£300M AI OS, won Amazon + Unilever); PMG's Alli (manages $6B media,
30+ data sources/client). The pattern: the proprietary tech moat is the most durable.

## Agency model data (2025) [fact — Promethean, AgencyAnalytics, Foxwell]

- 84% of agencies now identify as specialists (it's the baseline, not the edge)
- Specialists grow **43% faster**, margins **40–75%** vs generalists
- Avg agency spends only **7% of revenue** on sales/marketing (too low for growth targets)
- Retention: 8-figure agencies **92%** vs 7-figure 78%; 70% rate reporting "extremely
  important" for retention
- Pricing: hybrid retainer + performance is default above $20k/mo; min credible B2B =
  $15–25k/mo
- Recurring revenue >60% → sell at **2.2–3.5x revenue** vs 0.6–1.2x project-only
- AI labor compression **20–40%** on production; strategy hours reprice up, production down
- 85% of 8-figure agencies document SOPs for all core services

## The two winning 2025 models [fact — Promethean, tjrobertson]

1. **Hyper-specialized** — one vertical/channel, deep expertise, repeatable, premium
2. **Lean consultant-led network** — small core + specialist freelancer network, agility
   over headcount

BAZ is structurally #2 already (boutique, senior-partner, no junior pods). The moat build
is what converts it from "lean network" to "defensible systems shop."

## BAZ takeaway

The elite data is unambiguous: agencies that **institutionalize** the moat (tooling, SOPs,
repeatable revgen, productized offers) pull away; those that stay **founder-dependent**
plateau and sell cheap. BAZ's founder (the operator) + the AI builder (the agent) = the
two inputs to build the institutional moat without hiring. That is the play.
