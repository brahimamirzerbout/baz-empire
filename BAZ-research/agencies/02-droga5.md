# 02 — Droga5

> Bold, breakthrough creative that makes a cultural + commercial dent. Founded 2006 by
> David Droga (Australian creative); acquired by Accenture Song (2019).

## Bio [fact]

- Founded **2006** by **David Droga** (creative chairman); named after Droga + the NYC
  apartment he grew up in (#5)
- Grew to ~500+ staff; NYC HQ
- 2019: majority acquired by **Accenture Interactive** (now Accenture Song) — the
  consultancy-into-creative consolidation move

## Core lessons [fact]

1. **The idea leads, the format follows.** Droga5 didn't sell "a TV spot" or "a digital
   campaign" — it sold an *idea* that found its own medium (a stunt, a scavenger hunt, a
   $1 tap-water donation).
2. **Make a dent, not a deliverable.** Every piece aimed to influence culture, not just
   move a metric — and moving the metric was the *result* of the dent.
3. **Purpose + commerce can coexist** (Tap Project, Great Schlep, Honey Maid) — when
   authentic to the brand.
4. **A daisy chain of chapters.** Each piece of work led to the next opportunity (per
   Droga himself). Portfolio compounding, not one-off heroics.
5. **The acquisition playbook:** a creative boutique retains its edge inside a consultancy
   only if the culture is protected. Accenture-Droga5 is the test case for
   #2-of-L.E.K.-moats (specialized service expertise) absorbed into a platform.

## BAZ application

- **The idea leads, the format follows — at boutique scale.** BAZ doesn't sell "a paid
  media campaign" or "a website"; it sells the *idea* (instrumented, forecast-able
  growth), which then finds its medium (the diagnostic → the tracking stack → the
  retainer). The diagnostic *is* the idea finding its format.
- **Daisy-chain portfolio compounding:** each BAZ engagement should produce the case
  study + the mechanism that makes the next engagement easier. The moat product
  compounds: every client on the tracking stack makes the next diagnostic faster and the
  attribution data richer (network effect, per the SYNTAX "N").
- **Purpose + commerce, BAZ style:** BAZ's "we ship systems other agencies only
  storyboard" is a purpose framing that's also commercially differentiated — not
  philanthropy, but a stance. Authentic to the operator identity.
- **The acquisition caution:** BAZ should NOT sell to a consultancy/holdco prematurely —
  that's how the senior-partner culture dies. W+K's 2015 trust is the model: protect
  independence structurally. BAZ's value compounds while independent.
