## Core principles [fact]

1. **The Value Ladder.** Free/low → mid → high → continuity. Each rung ascends the
   customer; the low rungs liquidate ad spend, the high rungs hold the profit.
2. **Hook → Story → Offer.** At every rung: a hook that stops the scroll, a story that
   builds the relationship/belief, an offer so good it closes. Repeat the architecture,
   change the content.
3. **The funnel is the system.** Productize the customer journey into repeatable,
   testable steps. Attribution becomes possible because the path is defined.
4. **Who / Where / Bait.** Who is your dream customer, where do they hang out, what bait
   draws them into your funnel.
5. **Self-liquidating offers (SLO).** The front-end offer pays for the ad; the back-end
   holds the margin. (Converges with Hormozi's self-liquidating funnel.)
6. **The Perfect Webinar** structure (via Jason Fladlien): hook → teaching 3 secrets →
   transition → stack & pitch → objections.
7. **Build a mass movement:** a charismatic leader + a future-based cause + a new
   opportunity (not an "improvement" on the old).
8. **Publish or perish** — daily content is the fuel; the audience is the asset.

## BAZ application

- **Value Ladder = BAZ's tier architecture.** Diagnostic (low/free-ish) → Core
  ($6.5–9.5k, one channel) → Growth ($18–28k, full stack) → Project ($12–80k). The
  diagnostic is the SLO that liquidates the outbound effort and pre-sells the retainer.
- **Hook–Story–Offer applied to BAZ's own marketing:** Hook = "you can't scale what you
  can't measure, and 7 in 10 paid programs leak 15–30% attributed revenue." Story = the
  operator who ships systems other agencies storyboard. Offer = the diagnostic +
  tracking stack + 90-day plan, risk-reversed.
- **Funnel-as-system = BAZ's revgen engine:** define the path (outbound → diagnostic →
  proposal → retainer), instrument every step, optimize the bottleneck. This is Brunson
  + Hopkins (attribution) fused.
- **"New opportunity, not improvement":** BAZ doesn't sell "a better agency" — it sells a
  *new category* (the operator-who-ships). Brunson's mass-movement principle applied to
  positioning.
