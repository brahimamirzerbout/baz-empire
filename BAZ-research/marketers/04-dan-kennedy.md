## Core principles [fact]

1. **Direct response over brand awareness.** Brand awareness "doesn't earn you any
   revenue." Marketing must elicit a measurable response — a call, a click, a purchase.
   Everything trackable.
2. **The offer is everything.** Irresistible offer: value-stacked, risk-reversed,
   time-bound, with a clear response device. Most "marketing" fails because the offer is
   weak, not the copy.
3. **Market → Message → Media → Math.** The four M's. Pick the right market, match the
   message, choose the media, then do the math (cost per acquisition, lifetime value).
4. **Three-step / sequential follow-up.** The "3-letter system" — a measured cadence of
   follow-ups (not a single touch). Most money is in the follow-up.
5. **No discounts — scope down.** Never cheapen price; reduce scope instead. Discounting
   trains clients to negotiate every engagement.
6. **Free gifts + premiums + deadlines.** Bonus stacking + urgency drive action.
7. **Testimonials and proof as structural.** Not decoration — load-bearing.
8. **The customer list is the #1 asset** (echoes Halbert's "your own customers respond
   best").

## BAZ application

- **The offer is BAZ's highest-leverage lever.** BAZ's paid diagnostic
  ($3–7k Revenue Attribution Diagnostic) is a Kennedy-grade irresistible offer:
  fixed scope, clear deliverable, risk-reversed ("if we don't find a leak worth 10x the
  audit fee, you don't pay"), time-boxed 5 days. This is Kennedy's offer doctrine
  applied as a moat entry point.
- **Market → Message → Media → Math** maps directly to BAZ's "revenue not vanity" + the
  revgen engine. BAZ tracks CAC, payback, LTV — the Math leg most agencies skip.
- **No discounts, scope down:** already BAZ doctrine (never SaaS subscriptions; project +
  retainer pricing discipline). Hold the line on price.
- **Sequential follow-up:** BAZ outbound runs 10-12 day multi-touch sequences, not single
  emails. The breakup email (loss aversion) is Kennedy/Halbert lineage.
- **Bonus stacking on the Growth tier:** BAZ bundles tracking stack + reporting + operator
  agent as "free" value-stack components — Kennedy/Hormozi pattern.
