# 05 — Seth Godin

> Category design, smallest viable market, permission. The anti-interrupt marketer.
> BAZ lens trigger: **"Who are people like us, and what category are we creating?"**

## Bio [fact]

- Born 1960, Williamsville, NY
- Tufts (CS + philosophy), Stanford MBA
- Founded **Yoyodyne** (1995, permission marketing) → sold to Yahoo 1998 for $29.6M →
  Yahoo VP of Direct Marketing
- Founded Squidoo (2006, top-500 site) → sold to HubPages 2014
- Founded **altMBA** (2015)
- 20+ bestsellers in ~40 languages; daily blog (10,000+ posts, 1M readers/day)
- 5 TED talks (two most-watched of all time)
- Inducted into **both** the Direct Marketing Hall of Fame (2013) and the Marketing Hall
  of Fame (2018) — possibly the only person in both
- Podcast: *Akimbo* (top 1%)

## Core principles [fact]

1. **Don't market to the masses; market to the smallest viable market.** Serve a tribe so
   well that they tell the next tribe. Mass is dead (*We Are All Weird*).
2. **Permission, not interruption.** Earn the right to be heard. Anticipated + personal +
   relevant. The asset is the permission, not the ad spend.
3. **Be remarkable (Purple Cow).** Boring is invisible. Remarkability lives at the edges.
4. **Tell true stories that fit the audience's worldview.** "Culture beats truth." Stories
   need fertile ground. People buy the story, then the product.
5. **Pick yourself.** Don't wait to be chosen. Ship creative work (*The Practice*).
6. **The Dip.** Be the best in the world at a narrow thing — which means quitting the
   things you can't dominate.
7. **Tribes need leaders.** Connect people to each other and to an idea.
8. **Marketing as service** — "see, serve, connect," not sell.

## BAZ application

- **Category-of-one = BAZ's positioning answer.** When the user said "the vertical is me
  and my genius and my balls," that was a Godin move: don't pick a vertical, **claim a
  category nobody else can occupy** ("the operator who ships systems other agencies only
  storyboard"). This is *Purple Cow* + *The Dip* + category design in one.
- **Smallest viable market:** BAZ targets 30–50 named accounts (founders/CMOs who want
  the operator), not "MENA/EU/US businesses." Serve them so well they refer the next.
- **Permission over interruption:** BAZ inbound is the operator POV content (permission);
  outbound is founder-to-founder peer outreach (warm), not spray-and-pray. The email list
  is the asset.
- **The Dip applied:** BAZ quits the "generalist agency" race it can't win on price, and
  dominates the "senior-partner systems shop" niche where it can be best-in-world.
- **Tribes:** BAZ builds a community of operators-around-the-operator — the referral
  flywheel is the tribe, not the ad.
