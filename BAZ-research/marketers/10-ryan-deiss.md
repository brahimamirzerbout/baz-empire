## Core principles [best-practice]

1. **The Customer Value Journey (8 stages):**
   1. Aware → 2. Engage → 3. Subscribe → 4. Convert (low) → 5. Excite → 6. Ascend →
   7. Advocate → 8. Promote.
   Each stage is a single "next logical yes." Don't ask for the marriage on the first date.
2. **Ascension is engineered, not hoped for.** Build the ladder of offers; each step is
   small enough to be obvious.
3. **Lifecycle over campaign.** A campaign ends; a lifecycle compounds. Own the
   relationship across stages.
4. **Soap Opera Sequence (SOS):** a 3-5 email open-loop sequence that re-engages a new
   subscriber through story (set the drama → escalate → reveal). [best-practice, also
   attributed to Andre Chaperon's AutoResponder Madness lineage]
5. **Seinfeld emails:** pure-value, no-pitch content emails that keep the list warm
   between offers (named for "a show about nothing" — entertaining, consistent).
6. **The offer ladder + ascension paths** = the retention and LTV engine (converges with
   Brunson's value ladder + Kennedy's sequential follow-up).
7. **SOPs as a growth tool:** documented systems let the business scale beyond the
   founder (converges with Promethean's "85% of 8-figure agencies document SOPs").

## BAZ application

- **CVJ = BAZ's retention architecture.** BAZ doesn't stop at "closed." The 8 stages map
  to BAZ's client lifecycle: Aware (operator content) → Engage (outbound conversation)
  → Subscribe (diagnostic) → Convert (Core retainer) → Excite (early wins in first 30
  days) → Ascend (Core→Growth tier) → Advocate (case study) → Promote (referral flywheel).
  Each transition is an engineered "next logical yes."
- **Ascension drives NRR:** BAZ's Growth tier is the ascension target from Core; the
  hybrid performance kicker is the ascension incentive. 8-figure agencies retain 92% —
  the CVJ is *how* they do it.
- **SOPs as the non-founder moat:** BAZ productizes the 5-pillar catalog into fixed-scope
  SKUs + documented delivery SOPs so the moat survives the founder stepping back (L.E.K.
  institutional-relationship moat). Deiss's Scalable doctrine = BAZ's institutionalization.
- **Soap Opera Sequence for BAZ nurture:** new prospect → 3-email SOS that opens a loop
  (the leak they don't know about), escalates (the cost in $/month), reveals (the
  diagnostic). Then Seinfeld-style value emails between offers.
