# 01 — David Ogilvy

> "The father of advertising." Research-first, Big Idea, results over applause.
> BAZ lens trigger: **"What's the one big idea, and which headline won the test?"**

## Bio [fact]

- **Born** 23 June 1911, West Horsley, Surrey, England
- **Died** 21 July 1999, Touffou, France (aged 88)
- Founded **Ogilvy & Mather** (1948, as Hewitt, Ogilvy, Benson & Mather) with $6,000
- Path: chef (Hotel Majestic, Paris) → door-to-door AGA stove salesman → Gallup's
  Audience Research Institute (NJ) → British Intelligence (WWII, Camp X) → Amish farmer
  → adman at 38 with zero prior copywriting experience
- Wrote *The Theory and Practice of Selling the AGA Cooker* — Fortune called it the
  finest sales manual ever written
- Time (1962): "the most sought-after wizard in today's advertising industry"
- 1989: Ogilvy Group sold to WPP for $864M (hostile); Ogilvy became non-exec chairman

## Signature work [fact]

| Campaign | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Hathaway shirts — "The Man in the Hathaway Shirt" (eyepatch, 1951) | Story + image made a small Maine firm iconic; sold out demand |
| Schweppes — "The man from Schweppes is here" (Commander Whitehead, 1953) | 30M+ bottles/yr within 5 years; created the brand persona |
| Rolls-Royce — "At 60 mph the loudest noise is the electric clock" (1958) | Doubled US sales in a year; the most cited long-copy headline in history |
| Dove — "1/4 cleansing cream" | Positioning that built a multi-decade brand |
| Pepsodent, Shell, Sears, American Express, IBM | Built Ogilvy & Mather into a top-12 US agency |

## Core principles [fact]

1. **The Big Idea.** "It takes a big idea to attract attention." Without one, your ad
   passes like a ship in the night.
2. **Research is the floor.** Came from Gallup; billed himself as research director at
   launch. "I prefer the discipline of knowledge to the anarchy of ignorance."
3. **Results, not applause.** "In the modern world of business, it is useless to be a
   creative, original thinker unless you can also sell what you create." Late in life he
   praised *direct marketing* and pilloried branding people who "worship at the altar of
   creativity."
4. **The customer is not a moron, she's your wife** (1955). Treat the reader as
   intelligent.
5. **Long copy that sells.** Headlines do the heavy lifting; body copy closes. "On
   average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy."
6. **Test relentlessly.** Headlines, offers, layouts — never run unkeyed.
7. **Make the product the hero.** Image-building + information, not empty cleverness.
8. **Celebrities underperform.** Viewers remember the celebrity, forget the product.

## BAZ application

- **BAZ doctrine "revenue not vanity"** = Ogilvy's results-over-applause, verbatim. He
  was a direct-response partisan late in life — BAZ's tracking-is-the-moat is the
  spiritual descendant.
- **Big Idea test:** every BAZ pitch deck must name the one big idea before any tactic.
  If we can't state it in one sentence, the campaign isn't ready.
- **Headline-first delivery:** BAZ web/performance work leads with the headline that
  selects the right reader (echoes Halbert + Schwartz too).
- **Founder credibility = Ogilvy's "make the product the hero":** BAZ makes Brahim's
  shipped-systems the hero, not the agency logo.
- **Test discipline:** BAZ's "tracking is the moat" = Ogilvy's keyed coupons, modernized
  to server-side GTM + GA4 + CAPI. Same religion, new instruments.
