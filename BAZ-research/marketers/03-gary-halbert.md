# 03 — Gary C. Halbert

> "The Prince of Print." Market selection over copy quality. *The Boron Letters*.
> BAZ lens trigger: **"Are we selling to hungry people, and does the headline stop the scroll?"**

## Bio [fact]

- **Born** 19 January 1938, Barberton, Ohio
- **Died** 2007 (cerebral hemorrhage), aged 69
- Brash, brilliant, controversial; convicted of mail fraud → Federal Prison Camp, Boron, CA
- Wrote *The Boron Letters* to his son Bond from prison — now a marketing cult classic
- Published *The Gary Halbert Letter* — required reading for a generation of DR copywriters
- Son Bond Halbert maintains the archive (thegaryhalbertletter.com)

## Signature work [fact]

- The **coat-of-arms letter** — mailed ~600 million times; sold personalized family
  heraldry research. Made Halbert wealthy; became the foundational direct-mail case study.
- Dozens of controls across health, finance, info-products, collectibles

## Core principles [fact]

1. **The Starving Crowd.** Hamburger-stand thought experiment: give a competitor every
   advantage (better meat, location, price) — Halbert wants only one: **a starving
   crowd**. Market selection beats copy quality every time. "Become a student of markets,
   not products, not techniques, not copywriting."
2. **The list is the market.** 10 escalating list-quality levels (phone book → wealthy →
   mail-order buyers → repeat buyers of similar product → recent big-ticket buyers → hot
   for other mailers → **your own satisfied customers**, the best list of all).
3. **A-Pile / B-Pile.** People sort mail into A (personal, gets opened) and B (commercial
   junk, trashed unopened). First job: make your message look and feel like A-pile. →
   Digital translation: subject lines/creatives that read like a person, not a brand.
4. **Write to one person.** Not a demographic — one individual, at a kitchen table. Use
   "you" more than any other word. Match the reader's internal language.
5. **Research first, write second.** The research file should be thicker than the final
   copy. Find the "hidden benefit" competitors miss.
6. **Copywork by hand.** Transcribe winning ads word-for-word to absorb the rhythm.
7. **Readability = greased chute.** Short words, short sentences, short paragraphs, lots
   of white space. Read aloud; if you stumble, the reader stops.
8. **AIDA** + body is the heavy lifter once the envelope is opened.
9. **Swipe file discipline** — collect what works for pattern recognition, not plagiarism.

## BAZ application

- **Starving Crowd = BAZ's ICP-by-posture.** The "starving crowd" for BAZ = founders/CMOs
  already burned by a junior-pod agency. That hunger (proven by the industry data: 34%
  name acquisition pain, buyers skeptical of cold pitches) is the market. Don't sell to
  the unburned.
- **A-Pile translation to BAZ outbound:** cold email/LinkedIn that reads like a peer
  operator (Brahim → founder), not an agency pitch deck. The "invisible follow-up" —
  prospects Google you after a cold email — must show a strong personal brand, not a
  brochure. This is Halbert's A-pile in 2026.
- **Write-to-one applied to BAZ proposals:** every BAZ proposal addresses one named
  buyer in their language (VoC), not "Dear Client."
- **Copywork practice for BAZ operators:** maintain a swipe file of winning
  Hormozi/Brunson/Kennedy offers and BAZ's own winning decks; study structure, don't
  copy literally.
