# 02 — Eugene M. Schwartz

> The market strategist disguised as a copywriter. *Breakthrough Advertising* (1966).
> BAZ lens trigger: **"What awareness stage, and is the market saturated?"**

## Bio [fact]

- **Born** 18 March 1927, Butte, Montana
- Studied at University of Washington; moved to NYC 1949
- Joined Huber Hoge & Sons as a messenger → rose to copy chief
- Went independent 1954
- **85% hit ratio** on direct mail packages — unheard of in the field
- Worked for Boardroom, Rodale, Harry Lorayne, Cossman — the Who's Who of direct response
- Famous discipline: 33-minute-and-33-second timer, six sessions/day, 3 hrs/day. Could
  drink coffee or stare at the wall, but could not leave the chair.
- "I worked directly with Gene at Rodale until his death" — Pat Corpora, HCI Direct

## Signature work [fact]

- Wrote one ad that sold **1.98M copies of a single $25 book** (~$50M)
- "Read 300 Business Magazines in 30 Minutes" — launched Boardroom Reports ($3k start →
  $25M+ gross by 1985)
- "Give Me 15 Minutes and I'll Give You a Super-Power Memory" — launched Harry Lorayne
- "How Modern Chinese Medicine Helps... Burn Disease Out of Your Body..."
- "71-Year-Old Man Has Sexual Congress Five Times a Day!"

## Core principles [fact] — *Breakthrough Advertising*

### Part 1 — Strategy (diagnosis)

1. **Advertising does not create desire. It channels what already exists.** The single
   most important shift in the book. Stop asking "how do I make people want this?" → ask
   "what do they already want, and how does this genuinely help?"
2. **Five stages of awareness** (per prospect):
   - Unaware → Problem-aware → Solution-aware → Product-aware → Most-aware
   - Wrong awareness = wrong message = dead campaign. Most copy fails because it writes
     "most aware" (pitch) for a "problem aware" (cold) audience.
3. **Five stages of market sophistication** (per market):
   - Stage 1: be first (direct claim works)
   - Stage 2: enlarge the claim (competition appears)
   - Stage 3: introduce the mechanism (claims saturated → sell the *how*)
   - Stage 4: enlarge/name the mechanism (competitors copy mechanisms)
   - Stage 5: identification (talk to who the reader *is*; claims dead)
   - A Stage-1 headline in a Stage-5 market sounds naive; reverse is needlessly complex.
4. **Mass desire** = the public spread of a private want. Find the strongest existing
   desire your product can satisfy. Not the most interesting thing about the product —
   the most powerful thing the audience already wants.

### Part 2 — Seven techniques (execution)

Selection, Identification, Gradualization, Intensification, Redefinition, Mechanization,
Concentration. Build belief step by step; explain the mechanism; narrow attention onto
your product as the path. **Mechanization = transparency** in ethical modern use: show
your process, not a "secret formula."

## BAZ application

- **Awareness-stage diagnosis is BAZ's first deliverable question on every engagement:**
  "Where is this buyer on the 5-stage ladder, and is the market Stage 1 or Stage 5?"
  This decides headline strategy, funnel depth, and proof architecture before any copy.
- **BAZ operates in saturated markets** (MENA/EU/US agency space = Stage 4-5). Direct
  claims ("we grow revenue") are invisible. The leverage is **mechanism + identity** —
  BAZ's "tracking is the moat" is the mechanism; "the operator who ships" is the identity.
  That's a textbook Stage-4→5 move.
- **Don't create desire, channel it:** BAZ's ICP-by-posture ("wants the operator who
  ships, not the agency that decks") channels an existing desire (founders burned by
  junior-pod agencies) rather than manufacturing one.
- **Ethics guardrail:** Schwartz's tools can manipulate; BAZ uses mechanization as
  *transparency* (show the tracking stack, the 90-day plan, the real numbers) — not
  concealment. Aligns with BAZ's "never fabricate metrics" guardrail.
