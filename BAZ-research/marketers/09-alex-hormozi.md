## Core principles [fact]

### The Grand-Slam Offer

1. **Create an offer so good people feel stupid saying no.** Not discounting — stacking
   perceived value (the value equation: dream outcome × perceived likelihood of
   achievement ÷ time to achievement ÷ effort/sacrifice).
2. **Value equation** — increase dream outcome + perceived certainty, decrease time +
   effort/sacrifice.
3. **Risk reversal** — shift all risk to the seller. Conditional guarantees ("if you do X
   and don't see Y") beat blanket refunds (protects margin, creates urgency).
4. **Bonus stacking** — multiple modalities (software, call, AI, physical, digital).
5. **Scarcity + urgency** — but only *legitimate* (real deadlines, real capacity).

### Distribution / Leads

6. **Attention is manufactured, not discovered.** Build the audience; the audience is the
   asset.
7. **Self-liquidating funnel** — front-end pays for ads; back-end holds profit.
8. **The "Hydra"** — sell low/mid/high-ticket simultaneously so no money is left on the
   table from any segment of the audience.

### The 2025 launch playbook (operator extraction)

9. **Proof-before-promise sequence** — show frameworks/results, then reveal the offer.
10. **Mission-driven reframing** — turn a purchase into joining a movement ("donate 200
    books"). Us-vs-them identity (entrepreneurs vs the political record-holders).
11. **Give first, then segment** — massive free value to everyone, then the premium ask to
    the ready segment.
12. **Productize the expert as AI** — ACQ AI trained on $31M consulting data + frameworks.
    The expert scales without the expert's hours.
13. **The "Trophy Stack"** — reveal offer components one at a time with story, building
    perceived weight.
14. **Gamified goal tracking** — live counters unlocking community rewards turn buyers
    into co-pilots.

### Skip (ethical flags from the launch)

15. Fake FOMO ("live-only" that wasn't), misleading "special deals" that were public,
    weak content security (playbooks leaked). **BAZ guardrail: urgency must be real.**

## BAZ application

- **Grand-slam offer = BAZ's diagnostic + value stack.** The Revenue Attribution
  Diagnostic is the irresistible entry offer (10x guarantee, risk-reversed). The Growth
  tier bundles tracking stack + reporting + operator agent as a value stack, not a
  discount.
- **Productize the expert as AI = the BAZ Operator Agent.** Train an AI on Brahim's
  frameworks, 90-day plans, 5 pillars, doctrine. Sell access as a Growth-tier bonus /
  standalone. This is Hormozi's ACQ AI move at boutique scale — and it's the moat that
  works *without* the founder in the room (L.E.K. moat #1).
- **Self-liquidating outbound:** the diagnostic's found-value + retainer conversion
  liquidates the outbound effort. BAZ doesn't need $4M ad spend — it needs the diagnostic
  to convert at ≥40% to retainer.
- **Mission-driven reframing for BAZ:** "the operator you call when marketing needs to
  behave like a strategy team that actually ships" = the movement. Buyers aren't
  purchasing an agency; they're joining the "we ship" tribe (Cialdini Unity).
- **Ethical urgency only:** BAZ uses real capacity scarcity ("3 onboarding slots/quarter")
  and real deadlines, never fake FOMO. The Hormozi launch backlash is the cautionary tale.
