# Digital Marketing Fundamentals

## Definition

Digital marketing is the promotion of products, services, or brands through
internet-connected channels and technologies — search engines, social media,
email, websites, and mobile apps. The American Marketing Association defines it
broadly as "any marketing methods conducted through electronic devices which
utilize some form of a computer."

The defining difference from traditional marketing is **measurability**: nearly
every click, open, and purchase is trackable, turning marketing from guesswork
into a discipline.

## The durable fundamentals (they outlast tactics)

1. **Core channels** — SEO, content, social, email, paid ads, affiliate/influencer.
2. **The owned / earned / paid media model.**
3. **A data & measurement loop.**
4. **The customer funnel applied across digital touchpoints.**

Tools and platforms change; these foundations don't.

## The core channels

| Channel | What it does | Character |
| --- | --- | --- |
| SEO | Earn organic visibility when people search | Durable, high-trust, compounds (3–6+ mo) |
| Content marketing | Attract by being useful, not interruptive | Compounds; fuels other channels |
| Social media | Build audience, community, brand | Discovery + relationship |
| Email | Nurture an owned audience directly | Highest ROI (~$36–42 per $1) |
| Paid ads / PPC | Buy attention & traffic | Fast, controllable; stops with budget |
| Affiliate / influencer | Reach via partners' borrowed trust | Leverage others' audiences |

## Owned, Earned, Paid media

- **Owned** — your website, blog, email list, app. You control them; they compound.
- **Earned** — press, shares, reviews, word of mouth. Can't buy directly; most credible.
- **Paid** — ads across search/social/display. Fast and controllable but rented.

A strong strategy balances all three: paid rents demand, owned builds assets,
earned grants credibility.

## The data & measurement loop

- Visibility into what works lets you find and fix drop-off points.
- Modern principle: **"measure what matters, respect the customer, act on the
  data you can ethically collect"** — not "track everything" (privacy changes,
  cookie restrictions, regulation).
- Metrics that matter: CAC (customer acquisition cost), ROAS (return on ad
  spend), CPL (cost per lead), CLV/LTV (lifetime value), conversion rate.
- ROI formula: `(Revenue - Marketing Cost) / Marketing Cost × 100`.
- Rule of thumb benchmarks cited: aim for ~3:1 ROAS on paid, ~5:1 on organic.

## A starter framework

| Step | Focus | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Fundamentals | Understand channels, media model, data, funnel |
| 2 | One channel | Build real competence in a single discipline |
| 3 | A real project | Apply with actual stakes and feedback |
| 4 | Analytics | Set up measurement to learn what works |
| 5 | Integration | Add channels and connect them into one system |

## 8-step strategy framework

1. Define business objectives & SMART marketing goals.
2. Understand your audience deeply.
3. Audit your current digital presence.
4. Select your channel mix.
5. Develop your content strategy.
6. Set budget and allocate resources.
7. Implement tracking & attribution.
8. Launch, measure, and optimize.

## What's changing in 2026 (AI era)

- Discovery is going **visual and conversational**: Google AI Overviews (~16% of
  queries), ChatGPT search, Perplexity, Gemini, and platform-native search
  (TikTok, YouTube, Reddit).
- New optimization layers: **AEO** (Answer Engine Optimization — get cited by AI
  engines) and **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization — content shaped for AI
  synthesis).
- Privacy-first marketing and first-party data ownership are now essential.
- The fundamentals stay the same; execution becomes faster, more personalized,
  more visual.

## Typical result timelines

- Paid (PPC/social ads): leads within 24–48 hours.
- Social organic: engagement in days, conversion tuning 2–4 weeks.
- SEO: meaningful movement in 3–6 months, big gains 6–12 months.
- Email (existing list): immediate.
