# Paid Ads / PPC (Pay-Per-Click)

Primary focus: **Google Ads** campaign structure & best practices, plus general
PPC principles. PPC buys targeted traffic fast; it stops when the budget stops,
so it pairs with (doesn't replace) organic.

## What PPC is

You pay per click (or impression/action) to show ads on search engines, social
platforms, and display networks. Highest-intent traffic: people searching for
what you offer. Google Search Ads cited at ~4.4% avg conversion rate; organic
converts ~14.6% vs 1.7% for outbound.

## Google Ads account structure

Top-down hierarchy:

```
Account
└── Campaign        (budget, location, network, settings)
    └── Ad Group    (theme + related keywords + ads)
        └── Ads + Keywords
```

**Best practices**

- Organize as you create; reorganizing later wipes accumulated data/learning.
- Create a **separate campaign** when you need different budget or location
  targeting (e.g., shirts vs pants).
- One campaign can share budget/location across ad groups.
- Each **ad group = one narrow theme** with tightly related keywords.
- Mention at least one keyword in the ad headline for relevance.
- Keep **≥3 ads per ad group** and use optimized ad rotation to let Google pick
  the best per auction (signals: keyword, search term, device, location).

## Campaign types

Choose by objective:

- **Search** — text ads on search results (high intent).
- **Display** — image ads on websites.
- **Video** — video ads on YouTube.
- **Shopping** — product listings on Google.
- **Demand Gen** — ads within online feeds.
- **App** — promote your app.
- **Performance Max** — find high-value customers across all channels.
- **Local** — promote physical locations.

## Setup flow

1. Add business info (name, URL; link Business Profile / YouTube).
2. Choose advertising **objective & goal** → Google recommends campaign type.
3. Set budget & bidding.
4. Enter payment details.

## Core PPC mechanics to know

- **Keywords & match types** target searches; use negative keywords to cut waste.
- **Quality Score** (expected CTR, ad relevance, landing-page experience) drives
  cost & position — write relevant ads and send to strong landing pages.
- **Bidding**: manual or automated (e.g., maximize clicks/conversions, tCPA,
  tROAS). Start simple, then automate once data exists.
- **Conversion tracking** is mandatory — without it you can't optimize.
- **Search terms report** → refine keywords/negatives continuously.

## Measurement & targets

- ROAS (return on ad spend), CAC, CPL, CPA, CTR, conversion rate.
- Rule of thumb: aim for **≥3:1 ROAS** on paid channels.
- Run paid for fast pipeline; use organic (SEO, email, Business Profile) for
  compounding CPA.

## General PPC best practices (cross-platform)

- Set a clear budget and track every conversion.
- Test offers/creative in small batches; scale winners.
- Align ad → landing page message (consistency lifts conversion).
- Use retargeting to recapture interested users.
- Watch frequency to avoid ad fatigue.
