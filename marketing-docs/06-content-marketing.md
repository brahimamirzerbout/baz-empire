# Content Marketing

Content marketing attracts, engages, and converts audiences through genuinely
useful content (articles, guides, video, infographics) rather than interruptive
ads. It feeds SEO, social, and email, and compounds over time.

## Definition

A **documented plan** defining what content you'll create, for whom, through which
channels, and how you'll measure success — with every piece tied to a business
goal.

## The 6-component framework (CMI / 2026 synthesis)

1. **Strategic foundation** — goals/objectives (awareness, demand gen, pipeline,
   retention).
2. **Audience intelligence** — for B2B, map the full **buying committee** (a VP
   and a CTO have different needs); refresh personas annually.
3. **Content architecture** — 3–5 **content pillars** (buyer pain areas, not
   product categories) + topic clusters.
4. **Production & editorial process** — editorial calendar (themes over time) +
   content calendar (per piece: keyword, persona, funnel stage, owner, date).
5. **Distribution & promotion** — plan this *before* writing. Budget 25–35% of
   content spend on distribution for mature B2B.
6. **Measurement & analytics** — KPIs before copy.

> Only **47% of B2B marketers have a documented strategy** (CMI, 2025). Only ~3%
> of your market is buying now; build for the 97% still researching.

## Build it in 9 steps

1. Define goals.
2. Research audience (CRM data, sales calls, social analytics; map buying committee).
3. Run a **content audit** (inventory; tag keep/update/consolidate/remove).
4. Choose a CMS + governance model (who can publish, review, style).
5. Determine content types & formats.
6. Map content to the buyer journey (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU).
7. Build the distribution engine (plan "where will this live?" first).
8. Add the **AEO layer** (see below).
9. Implement measurement from day one.

## Funnel-stage content

- **TOFU (Awareness)**: educational blog, explainers, newsletters — identify problems.
- **MOFU (Consideration)**: guides, comparisons, webinars, case-study lite.
- **BOFU (Decision)**: customer success stories w/ quantified outcomes, security/
  compliance docs, implementation roadmaps, competitive displacement, pricing.

## The AEO layer (2026)

~45% of B2B buyers use AI for initial research; 67% prefer rep-free buying.
Tactics:

- Lead each page with a **direct declarative answer**.
- Build **entity density** (name specific tools, frameworks, companies).
- Make **H2 headings answer specific questions**.
- Treat **FAQ sections as strategic citation assets** for AI engines.

## Distribution (B2B ranked by ICP precision)

Owned media (website, email, LinkedIn) first → niche industry media → webinar
co-hosting → content syndication → LinkedIn paid → community seeding →
partnerships. Add AEO as a mandatory layer.

## Repurposing = extraction, not resizing

Mine a long-form source (e.g., a 45-min expert conversation) into: short video
clips, LinkedIn posts, newsletter snippets, FAQ sections, a blog post. This yields
**3–5x reach at 20–30% of net-new cost**.

## Measurement (CMI 5-stage model)

1. **See Us** — reach, traffic, AI visibility.
2. **Connect** — engagement, time on page, email CTR.
3. **Trust Us** — returning-visitor rate, webinar attendance (most teams skip trust).
4. **Choose Us** — lead forms, content-assisted conversions.
5. **Champion Us** — retention, referrals.

Add **human-input measurement** (advisory boards, win/loss interviews) — analytics
show content was *consumed*; conversations show it *mattered*.

## Common mistakes

- Optimizing for traffic instead of pipeline.
- Publishing with no distribution plan.
- Building for one persona in a committee buying process.
- Ignoring AI search as a distribution channel.
- Scaling production before proving quality.
- Measuring only what's easy to measure.
