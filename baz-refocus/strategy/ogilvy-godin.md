# Ogilvy × Godin — The Collaboration

Two men who rarely agree. One built the 360° brand system; the other told you to
ignore everyone but the weirdos who already get it. Applied to BAZ, they stop
fighting — because each fixes the other's blind spot.

## The tension, stated plainly
- **Godin** says: pick the smallest viable audience, be remarkable, build a tribe,
  earn permission. His weakness: "remarkable" alone doesn't pay rent — you still
  need craft, consistency, and a brand that survives contact with the world.
- **Ogilvy** says: the consumer isn't a moron, she's your wife. Craft the big idea,
  sweat every touchpoint, build a 360° brand experience. His weakness: at BAZ's
  scale, 360° everywhere is exactly the extensity Godin warns against.

## The deal they struck (for BAZ)
> **Godin chooses the ONE. Ogilvy crafts it to world-class, then wraps it in a 360°
> experience — but only for that one tribe, on that one channel, with that one offer.**

Ogilvy's discipline, Godin's scope. That's the whole collaboration.

| Question | Godin decides | Ogilvy executes |
|---|---|---|
| Who? | Smallest viable audience: technical founders | Build the brand voice *for them*, in their language |
| What? | One hero offer (45-day system) | The "big idea" + message hierarchy around it |
| Where? | One channel (email + LinkedIn) | A 360° *experience* across the few touchpoints that tribe actually hits |
| How good? | Remarkable or nothing | Craft every word, every asset to Ogilvy standard |
| Scale? | Tribe first, then a second | Only expand the 360° after the tribe compounds |

## What we take from Ogilvy (and what we reject)
From your own `bazventures-org-chart.md`: "Never copy Ogilvy's regional matrix —
irrelevant below global-enterprise scale." Agreed. So:

**Adopt from Ogilvy:**
- The *Big Idea* — one thought the whole brand hangs on ("Intensity beats extensity").
- *Craft* — "don't sell the steak, sell the sizzle"; every asset earns its place.
- The *360° touchpoint map* — but scoped to the tribe's actual journey, not the planet.
- *Positioning as a sentence* — "BAZ is the only agency that builds your whole
  marketing system in 45 days, senior-only, or you don't pay."

**Reject from Ogilvy:**
- Regional matrix, junior layers, account pyramids. (Per your org chart — keep it flat.)
- "Be everywhere." Ogilvy's 360° is for Coca-Cola. BAZ's 360° is for one tribe.

## The result
A brand that is *focused like Godin* and *crafted like Ogilvy* — the purple cow,
built by the man who taught the world to make cows people remember.
